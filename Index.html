📘 Alliance structure

How we play

Three teams, one system

The alliance does not move as one giant blob. It is divided into three coordinated teams that operate at the same time without losing structure. Pushes, holds, and rotations are executed through this system.

  • Team 1 handles main attack and pressure.
  • Team 2 handles holding and structure defense.
  • Team 3 handles reinforcement, pressure, and secondary objectives.
Rules

Discipline is required

  • Follow assigned team first.
  • Do not leave position unless instructed.
  • Do not start random rallies.
  • Reinforce early, not after a structure is already failing.
  • After capture, fill immediately and secure it before moving on.
Leadership

Command structure

Overall direction is controlled centrally, while each team stays focused on its assigned function. This keeps the alliance fast, clear, and difficult to break under pressure.

⚔️ Battle plans

Defending Alliance Center

Alliance Center is to be defended in layers, not as one pile.

  • Team 2 starts as the main hold team inside the Alliance Center.
  • Team 1 stays outside and punishes enemy rally leaders, exposed marches, and overextensions.
  • Team 3 floats between Alliance Center and nearby lanes to reinforce, cut refill, and stabilize pressure points.
  • Damaged players are to be rotated out instead of weak overfill.
  • Reinforcements are to be timed properly before enemy impact, not too early and not too late.
Attacking Alliance Center

Alliance Center is to be taken in sequence and then secured immediately.

  • Team 1 starts the main rally chain.
  • Team 3 supports the push, follows pressure, and blocks enemy refill lanes.
  • Team 2 fills immediately once control is taken so the capture becomes stable.
  • No wasted solo hits when rally fill and fast reinforcement are needed.
  • The alliance must be ready for the counterattack the second the structure is captured.
Trade Posts

Trade Posts are to be used for control, not chaos.

  • Team 2 can hold one post.
  • Team 3 can hold or pressure the second post.
  • Team 1 roams, hits the next target, or helps break the hardest resistance.
  • After capture, the assigned hold team fills immediately while the attack team moves on.
  • If one side starts collapsing, Team 3 becomes the first rotation team to stabilize it.
  • Stable control is preferred over messy overextension.
